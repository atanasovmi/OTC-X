\chapter{Beweggründe — Motivations \& Vision}
\label{ch:beweggruende}

\section{The Genesis of an Idea}

The Swiss \gls{otcx} market harbours a paradox that has long eluded the scrutiny it deserves: here lies a segment of the financial landscape encompassing \textbf{243 unlisted equities}, yet it remains cloaked in an opacity that defies modern standards of market transparency.  For institutional actors — traders, compliance officers, risk managers — the absence of consolidated liquidity intelligence is not merely an inconvenience; it is a structural impediment to sound decision-making.

This project was conceived from a singular, compelling observation: \emph{the data exists, but the signal does not}.  Raw trade records, scattered across fragmented sources, offered no coherent narrative about liquidity conditions, price-impact dynamics, or anomalous market behaviour.  The ambition, therefore, was unequivocal — to architect an end-to-end intelligence platform that transforms disparate trade data into a \textbf{transparent, signal-rich radar} for the \gls{bekb} Trading Desk.

\begin{insightbox}
\textbf{Core Vision:}\; Turn the opaque Swiss OTC market into an automated, continuously updated liquidity radar — surfacing anomalies, scoring risk, and empowering informed action through 26 quantitative metrics and 11 interactive visualisations.
\end{insightbox}

\section{The Problem Space}

The Swiss OTC market, administered through the Berner Börse, presents several characteristics that render conventional analysis inadequate:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textcolor{SwissRed}{\textbf{\arabic*.}}]
  \item \textbf{Fragmentation}\;— Trade data is dispersed across individual security endpoints with no unified view.
  \item \textbf{Illiquidity}\;— Many securities trade only sporadically; standard exchange metrics (VWAP, continuous order books) are inapplicable.
  \item \textbf{Opacity}\;— No publicly available liquidity scoring, no anomaly alerting, no historical baseline comparison.
  \item \textbf{Scale}\;— 243 securities spanning 20+ years of trading history amount to approximately 133\,000 individual trade records.
  \item \textbf{Heterogeneity}\;— Sectors range from banking and insurance to industry and real estate, each exhibiting distinct liquidity profiles.
\end{enumerate}

\section{What I Learned}

Building OTC-X was, in every meaningful sense, a journey through the full spectrum of data engineering and quantitative finance.  Three lessons stand out with particular clarity:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textcolor{MetricBlue}{\textbf{(\roman*)}}]
  \item \textbf{Domain intimacy precedes technical elegance.}\;Understanding the microstructure of OTC markets — why the Amihud ratio matters, how off-book trades distort volume signals, why calendar days are misleading for rolling baselines — was the prerequisite that shaped every architectural decision.
  \item \textbf{Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication.}\;The four-stage pipeline design emerged not from a desire for minimalism, but from the recognition that each transformation stage must be independently testable, recoverable, and explainable.  Fifty-eight tests validate not just correctness, but also the \emph{intent} behind each computation.
  \item \textbf{Visualisation is not decoration; it is argumentation.}\;The eleven chart types in the dashboard are not ornamental.  Each one answers a specific analytical question — from the 3D market explorer that reveals hidden clustering to the anomaly treemap that converts numerical scores into spatial intuition.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Future Outlook — Weiterführung}

The current iteration of OTC-X establishes a robust foundation.  The trajectory for continued development encompasses three strategic pillars:

\begin{description}[style=nextline, leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item[\textcolor{SwissRed}{Pillar I — Predictive Analytics}]
  Integration of time-series forecasting (ARIMA, Prophet) to project liquidity conditions forward, enabling the trading desk to anticipate illiquidity events before they materialise.
  \item[\textcolor{SwissRed}{Pillar II — Cross-Market Enrichment}]
  Incorporating SIX Swiss Exchange reference data and macroeconomic indicators (SNB interest rates, CPI) to contextualise OTC movements within the broader Swiss financial ecosystem.
  \item[\textcolor{SwissRed}{Pillar III — Real-Time Streaming}]
  Transitioning from daily batch processing to near-real-time ingestion using WebSocket-based data feeds, reducing signal latency from 24 hours to minutes.
\end{description}

\begin{insightbox}
The ultimate aspiration is to render OTC-X not merely a retrospective analytical tool, but a \textbf{forward-looking decision-support system} — a sentinel that watches the Swiss OTC market so that traders need not.
\end{insightbox}

\section{Functional Requirements}

\begin{requirement}{FR-01 \;\textbar\; Automated Security Discovery\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The system shall automatically discover and catalogue all actively traded securities on the OTC-X platform, extracting their ISIN, Valor number, name, and sector classification.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;A complete and current securities universe is the prerequisite for all downstream analytics.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;The output file \texttt{securities.csv} contains $\geq 240$ valid entries with no duplicate ISINs.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{backend/operations/soft\_crawl.py}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{FR-02 \;\textbar\; Historical Trade Retrieval\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The system shall retrieve the complete trade history for every catalogued security from the OTC-X \gls{api}, including date, time, price, volume, and off-book indicator.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;Historical depth (20+ years) enables robust baseline computation and long-term trend analysis.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;Individual CSV files per ISIN are produced; total trade count $\geq 130{,}000$; ISIN-level integrity verified through checksums.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{backend/operations/fetcher.py}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{FR-03 \;\textbar\; Data Consolidation \& Quality Assurance\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The system shall consolidate all individual trade files into a single, deduplicated, type-safe master dataset stored in Apache Parquet format with Zstandard compression.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;Columnar storage with compression minimises I/O cost and ensures schema consistency for the metrics engine.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;\texttt{master\_trades.parquet} is produced; zero duplicate rows; all columns conform to the prescribed schema (Isin: String, Datum: Date, Zeit: Utf8, Kurs: Float64, Volumen: Float64, Off Book: Int8).\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{backend/operations/build\_master\_parquet.py}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{FR-04 \;\textbar\; Daily Metrics Computation (26 Columns)\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;For each ISIN and each trading day, the system shall compute 26 liquidity metrics encompassing trade intensity, volume, price dynamics, volatility, illiquidity ratios, rolling baselines, and anomaly flags.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;The 26-metric schema constitutes the analytical core; it transforms raw trades into risk-relevant signals.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;\texttt{daily\_metrics.parquet} produced with exactly 26 columns; schema validated against reference definition (see Appendix~\ref{app:schema}); rolling medians computed over trading-day windows.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{backend/operations/metrics.py}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{FR-05 \;\textbar\; Anomaly Detection \& Scoring\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The system shall detect anomalous trading activity through three binary flags (volume spike, activity spike, price gap) and compute a weighted composite anomaly score on the integer range $[0,\,7]$.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;Automated anomaly detection enables the trading desk to prioritise attention on securities exhibiting unusual behaviour.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;Anomaly scores correctly computed as $S = 3 \cdot \mathbb{1}_{[\mathrm{vol}]} + 2 \cdot \mathbb{1}_{[\mathrm{act}]} + 2 \cdot \mathbb{1}_{[\mathrm{gap}]}$; scores $\in \{0, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7\}$.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{backend/operations/metrics.py :: compute\_anomaly\_flags()}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{FR-06 \;\textbar\; Interactive Dashboard (4 Tabs, 11 Charts)\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The system shall present a Streamlit-based web dashboard with four purpose-built tabs (Overview, Market Data, Analytics, Anomaly Monitor) delivering 11 interactive Plotly chart types.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;Visual intelligence is the primary consumption modality for the trading desk; interactivity enables ad-hoc exploration.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;Dashboard loads within 5 seconds; all 11 chart types render without error; sector filtering, search, and CSV export functional.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{frontend/app.py}, \texttt{frontend/operations/charts.py}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{FR-07 \;\textbar\; Security Deep-Dive with $\sigma$-Band Chart\hfill\textcolor{white}{Should}}
\textbf{Description:}\;For any selected ISIN, the dashboard shall display a historical price chart with a rolling 30-day standard deviation band ($\pm 1\sigma$) overlaid on the price series, alongside a volume subplot.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;Per-security drill-down enables granular assessment of individual liquidity conditions and volatility regimes.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;$\sigma$-band correctly computed from rolling 30-day price standard deviation; band renders as shaded fill; volume bars below.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{frontend/operations/charts.py :: chart\_security\_history()}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{FR-08 \;\textbar\; 3D Market Explorer\hfill\textcolor{white}{Could}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The Analytics tab shall provide a five-dimensional interactive 3D scatter plot, allowing the user to map any combination of 12 metric options to the X, Y, Z axes, colour, and size dimensions.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Rationale:}\;Multi-dimensional exploration reveals latent market structure invisible in two-dimensional projections.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Acceptance Criterion:}\;All five dimension selectors functional; log$_{10}$ transform toggle operational; outlier removal (1st–99th percentile) correctly filters data.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{frontend/app.py} (Analytics tab, 3D Explorer section)
\end{requirement}

\section{Non-Functional Requirements}

\begin{requirement}{NFR-01 \;\textbar\; Performance\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The complete 4-stage pipeline shall execute within 5 minutes on standard CI hardware (GitHub Actions, 2~vCPU, 7~GB RAM).  Dashboard page load shall not exceed 5 seconds.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Metric:}\;Pipeline wall-clock time $\leq 300$\,s; Streamlit initial render $\leq 5$\,s.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;Pipeline receipt logs (\texttt{backend/logs/pipeline\_receipt\_*.log})
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{NFR-02 \;\textbar\; Reliability \& Recoverability\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;Each pipeline stage shall be independently recoverable.  Failure in any stage shall halt subsequent stages, produce a diagnostic receipt log, and preserve all previously completed artefacts.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Metric:}\;Receipt log generated even on failure; no data corruption on partial run.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{backend/pipeline.py :: \_run\_stage()}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{NFR-03 \;\textbar\; Test Coverage\hfill\textcolor{white}{Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;The system shall maintain a minimum of 50 automated tests covering path resolution, backend operations, frontend utilities, and chart rendering.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Metric:}\;$\geq 58$ tests passing (current baseline); all tests executed via \texttt{python -m pytest tests/ -v}.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{tests/test\_backend.py}, \texttt{tests/test\_frontend.py}, \texttt{tests/test\_paths.py}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{NFR-04 \;\textbar\; Data Integrity \& Compression\hfill\textcolor{white}{\small Must}}
\textbf{Description:}\;All persistent data artefacts shall use Apache Parquet format with either Snappy or Zstandard compression, ensuring both storage efficiency and schema fidelity.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Metric:}\;Parquet files readable without schema override; compression ratio $\geq 3\times$ vs.\ raw CSV.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;\texttt{backend/operations/build\_master\_parquet.py},\\ \texttt{backend/operations/metrics.py}
\end{requirement}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{requirement}{NFR-05 \;\textbar\; Maintainability \& Documentation\hfill\textcolor{white}{Should}}
\textbf{Description:}\;All Python modules shall employ type hints on function signatures and NumPy-style docstrings with Parameters, Returns, and optional Notes sections.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Metric:}\;100\% of public functions annotated; docstrings present on all 12 Python modules.\\[4pt]
\textbf{Trace:}\;All \texttt{*.py} files
\end{requirement}

\section{Constraints}

\begin{constraintbox}{C-01 \;\textbar\; Data Sovereignty}
All data processing shall comply with the authorisation granted by \gls{bekb} (Schwarzenburgstrasse~160, 3001~Bern).  Data sovereignty remains with the project author until formal handover.  No third-party data egress permitted.
\end{constraintbox}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{constraintbox}{C-02 \;\textbar\; Technology Stack}
The system is constrained to: Python~3.10+, Polars (ETL), Pandas/NumPy (analytics), Streamlit/Plotly (dashboard), Apache Parquet (storage), GitHub Actions (CI/CD), and pytest (testing).  No additional runtime dependencies without explicit approval.
\end{constraintbox}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{constraintbox}{C-03 \;\textbar\; Deployment Model}
The dashboard shall be deployable via Streamlit Community Cloud.  The backend pipeline shall execute within GitHub Actions runners.  No proprietary cloud infrastructure required.
\end{constraintbox}

\section{The Four-Stage ETL Pipeline}

The pipeline is orchestrated by \texttt{pipeline.py}, which sequentially executes four stages with full output capture, timing instrumentation, and comprehensive receipt logging.

\subsection{Stage 1 — Valor Extraction (\texttt{soft\_crawl.py})}

The pipeline commences with a non-intrusive crawl of the OTC-X platform to discover the complete securities universe.  For each security, the following attributes are extracted:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{ISIN} — International Securities Identification Number (12-character alphanumeric)
  \item \textbf{Valor} — Swiss-specific security identifier
  \item \textbf{Name} — Company name
  \item \textbf{Sektor} — Economic sector classification
\end{itemize}

The output, \texttt{securities.csv}, serves as the authoritative registry against which all subsequent stages operate.

\subsection{Stage 2 — Trade Data Retrieval (\texttt{fetcher.py})}

For every ISIN in the securities registry, the fetcher retrieves the complete trade history from the OTC-X API.  Key implementation details include:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Retry logic} with exponential backoff for transient network failures
  \item \textbf{Rate limiting} to respect API quotas and prevent throttling
  \item \textbf{ISIN validation} using the ISO~6166 check-digit algorithm
  \item \textbf{Logging} — each download session generates a timestamped log file in \texttt{backend/logs/}
\end{itemize}

Individual CSV files are produced per ISIN in \texttt{backend/data/trades/}, preserving the raw data for audit purposes.

\subsection{Stage 3 — Data Consolidation (\texttt{build\_master\_parquet.py})}

The consolidation stage merges 243 individual CSV files into a single, type-safe master dataset:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Schema enforcement} — columns cast to prescribed types (Date, Float64, Int8)
  \item \textbf{Deduplication} — exact-match row deduplication eliminates retransmitted records
  \item \textbf{Compression} — output written as \texttt{master\_trades.parquet} with Zstandard compression
  \item \textbf{Validation} — row counts, ISIN counts, and null checks reported
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Stage 4 — Metrics Engine (\texttt{metrics.py})}

The metrics engine — the quantitative heart of OTC-X — transforms the master trade dataset into a 26-column analytical layer.  This stage is detailed exhaustively in Chapter~\ref{ch:metrics}.

\section{Pipeline Orchestration \& Receipt Logging}

The orchestrator (\texttt{pipeline.py}) employs a custom \texttt{\_TeeStream} class that mirrors all standard output to both the console and an internal buffer.  Upon completion, a comprehensive receipt is assembled containing:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Per-stage status} — success/failure with elapsed time
  \item \textbf{Data product statistics} — row counts, ISIN counts, file sizes
  \item \textbf{Anomaly snapshot} — distribution of anomaly scores across the latest trading day
  \item \textbf{Verbatim stage output} — complete stdout capture per stage
\end{itemize}

Receipts are written to \texttt{backend/logs/pipeline\_receipt\_\{timestamp\}.log} and committed to version control, providing a full audit trail of every pipeline execution.

\chapter[Metrics Engine — Mathematische Grundlagen]{Metrics Engine —\\Mathematische Grundlagen}
\label{ch:metrics}

This chapter constitutes the scientific core of the document.  Every metric computed by \texttt{metrics.py} is presented with its mathematical definition, implementation rationale, and interpretation within the context of OTC market microstructure.

The metrics engine executes a four-step transformation pipeline:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textcolor{SwissRed}{\textbf{Step \arabic*:}}, leftmargin=3.5em]
  \item \textbf{Daily Aggregation} — group trades by ISIN $\times$ date
  \item \textbf{Liquidity Metrics} — derive price dynamics, illiquidity, and spread proxies
  \item \textbf{Rolling Baselines} — compute 30-trading-day medians per ISIN
  \item \textbf{Anomaly Detection} — flag deviations and compute composite scores
\end{enumerate}

% ─── STEP 1 ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Step 1: Daily Aggregation}

Raw trades are grouped by $(\text{ISIN},\, \text{Date})$ to produce one observation per security per trading day.  Let $\mathcal{T}_{i,d}$ denote the set of trades for security $i$ on date $d$, with $|\mathcal{T}_{i,d}| = n$ trades.

\begin{formulabox}{Trade Intensity}
\[
  N_{i,d} = |\mathcal{T}_{i,d}|
\]
The number of trades executed for security $i$ on date $d$.  Stored as \texttt{trades\_today} (\texttt{UInt32}).
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Volume — Units \& CHF Value}
\[
  V_{i,d}^{\text{units}} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} v_k
  \qquad\qquad
  V_{i,d}^{\text{CHF}} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} v_k \cdot p_k
\]
where $v_k$ and $p_k$ are the volume (units) and price of the $k$-th trade, respectively.\\[4pt]
\texttt{volume\_today\_units} (Float64)\;\;\textbar\;\;\texttt{volume\_today\_chf} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Price Bounds \& Reference Prices}
\[
  P_{i,d}^{\min} = \min_{k} \, p_k
  \qquad
  P_{i,d}^{\max} = \max_{k} \, p_k
  \qquad
  P_{i,d}^{\text{first}} = p_1
  \qquad
  P_{i,d}^{\text{last}} = p_n
\]
Trades are sorted chronologically by time within each day before selecting the first and last prices.\\[4pt]
\texttt{price\_min}, \texttt{price\_max}, \texttt{price\_first}, \texttt{price\_last} (all Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Intraday Volatility}
\[
  \sigma_{i,d} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(p_k - \bar{p}\right)^2}
  \qquad\text{where}\qquad
  \bar{p} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^{n} p_k
\]
The sample standard deviation of all intraday prices.  For single-trade days ($n=1$), $\sigma_{i,d} = 0$.\\[4pt]
\texttt{volatility\_daily} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Maximum Trade Duration}
Let $\tau_1 < \tau_2 < \cdots < \tau_n$ be the trade timestamps (in minutes since midnight):
\[
  \Delta\tau_{i,d}^{\max} = \max_{k \in \{2,\ldots,n\}} \left(\tau_k - \tau_{k-1}\right)
\]
The longest gap between consecutive trades within the day (in minutes).  For single-trade days, $\Delta\tau_{i,d}^{\max} = 0$.\\[4pt]
\texttt{trade\_duration\_min} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Off-Book Percentage}
\[
  \text{OB}_{i,d} = \frac{\sum_{k=1}^{n} \mathbb{1}_{[\text{off-book}]}(k)}{n} \times 100
\]
The proportion of trades executed off the central order book, expressed as a percentage.\\[4pt]
\texttt{off\_book\_pct} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

% ─── STEP 2 ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Step 2: Liquidity Metrics}

Four derived metrics capture the liquidity characteristics of each security on each trading day.

\begin{formulabox}{Price Change (\%)}
\[
  \Delta P_{i,d} = \frac{P_{i,d}^{\text{last}} - P_{i,d}^{\text{first}}}{P_{i,d}^{\text{first}}} \times 100
\]
The percentage change from the opening to the closing price.  Null and NaN values are replaced with $0.0$.\\[4pt]
\texttt{price\_change\_pct} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Log Returns}
\[
  r_{i,d} = \ln\!\left(\frac{P_{i,d}^{\text{last}}}{P_{i,d}^{\text{first}}}\right)
\]
The natural logarithm of the intraday price ratio.  Log returns possess the desirable property of \emph{time-additivity}: $r_{[t_1, t_3]} = r_{[t_1, t_2]} + r_{[t_2, t_3]}$, making them the preferred return measure in quantitative finance.\\[4pt]
\texttt{log\_returns} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Spread Proxy — Corwin–Schultz Estimator}
\[
  S_{i,d}^{\text{HL}} = \ln\!\left(\frac{P_{i,d}^{\max}}{P_{i,d}^{\min}}\right)
\]
This high-low spread estimator, inspired by the Corwin--Schultz (2012) methodology, serves as a proxy for the bid-ask spread in the absence of explicit order-book data.  A wider spread indicates higher implicit transaction costs and, by extension, lower liquidity.\\[4pt]
\texttt{spread\_log\_hl} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Amihud Illiquidity Ratio ($\lambda$)}
\[
  \lambda_{i,d} =
  \begin{cases}
    \displaystyle\frac{|r_{i,d}|}{V_{i,d}^{\text{CHF}}} \times 10^{6} & \text{if } V_{i,d}^{\text{CHF}} > 0 \\[8pt]
    \text{null} & \text{if } V_{i,d}^{\text{CHF}} = 0
  \end{cases}
\]
The Amihud (2002) illiquidity ratio quantifies the price impact per unit of trading volume.  A higher $\lambda$ indicates that a security's price is more sensitive to traded volume — the hallmark of an illiquid asset.\\[4pt]
The scaling factor $10^6$ normalises the ratio to a human-readable range.  Division by zero is explicitly guarded against.\\[4pt]
\texttt{amihud\_daily} (Float64)
\end{formulabox}

\begin{insightbox}
\textbf{Why Amihud?}\;In the OTC-X market, where continuous order books are absent and trades are sporadic, the Amihud ratio provides a robust, model-free measure of illiquidity that requires only price and volume data — both of which are available from trade records.
\end{insightbox}

% ─── STEP 3 ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Step 3: Rolling Baselines (30 Trading Days)}

To distinguish genuine anomalies from normal variation, the engine computes rolling medians over a window of 30 \emph{trading days} per ISIN.  The critical distinction is that these are not calendar days — each row represents a day on which the security actually traded.

\begin{formulabox}{Rolling Median}
For any metric $x$ and window size $w = 30$:
\[
  \tilde{x}_{i,t}^{(w)} = \operatorname{median}\!\left(x_{i,t-w+1},\; x_{i,t-w+2},\; \ldots,\; x_{i,t}\right)
\]
where the indices refer to the $w$ most recent trading days for security $i$.\\[6pt]
\textbf{Implementation detail:}\;\texttt{min\_samples=1} ensures that newly listed securities with fewer than 30 trading sessions still receive a baseline estimate from whatever data is available.
\end{formulabox}

Four baselines are computed:

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Rolling baseline columns}
\label{tab:baselines}
\rowcolors{2}{LightSilver}{white}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l l X}
\toprule
\textbf{Baseline Column} & \textbf{Source} & \textbf{Interpretation} \\
\midrule
\texttt{trades\_30d\_median}     & \texttt{trades\_today}      & Typical daily trade count over the last 30 sessions \\
\texttt{volume\_30d\_median}     & \texttt{volume\_today\_chf}  & Typical daily CHF volume over the last 30 sessions \\
\texttt{volatility\_30d\_median} & \texttt{volatility\_daily}   & Typical intraday volatility over the last 30 sessions \\
\texttt{amihud\_30d\_median}     & \texttt{amihud\_daily}       & Typical illiquidity level over the last 30 sessions \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\begin{insightbox}
\textbf{Why medians rather than means?}\;The median is robust to outliers — a single extreme trading day (e.g., a corporate event) will not distort the baseline.  This is particularly important in OTC markets where the distribution of daily volumes and price changes is heavily right-skewed.
\end{insightbox}

% ─── STEP 4 ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Step 4: Anomaly Detection}

The anomaly detection module converts baseline deviations into a structured, interpretable scoring system.

\subsection{Binary Anomaly Flags}

Three independent binary flags are evaluated for each observation:

\begin{formulabox}{Volume Spike}
\[
  \mathbb{1}_{[\text{vol\_spike}]} =
  \begin{cases}
    1 & \text{if } V_{i,d}^{\text{CHF}} > 1.5 \times \tilde{V}_{i,d}^{(30)} \\
    0 & \text{otherwise}
  \end{cases}
\]
Triggered when today's CHF volume exceeds 150\% of the 30-trading-day rolling median.
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Activity Spike}
\[
  \mathbb{1}_{[\text{act\_spike}]} =
  \begin{cases}
    1 & \text{if } N_{i,d} > 1.5 \times \tilde{N}_{i,d}^{(30)} \\
    0 & \text{otherwise}
  \end{cases}
\]
Triggered when today's trade count exceeds 150\% of the 30-trading-day rolling median.
\end{formulabox}

\begin{formulabox}{Price Gap}
\[
  \mathbb{1}_{[\text{price\_gap}]} =
  \begin{cases}
    1 & \text{if } |\Delta P_{i,d}| > 5\% \\
    0 & \text{otherwise}
  \end{cases}
\]
Triggered when the absolute intraday price change exceeds the 5\% threshold.
\end{formulabox}

\subsection{Composite Anomaly Score}

The three binary flags are combined into a single weighted score:

\begin{formulabox}{Anomaly Score $S \in [0,\,7]$}
\[
  \boxed{\;S_{i,d} = 3 \cdot \mathbb{1}_{[\text{vol\_spike}]} + 2 \cdot \mathbb{1}_{[\text{act\_spike}]} + 2 \cdot \mathbb{1}_{[\text{price\_gap}]}\;}
\]
\textbf{Weight rationale:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Volume spike (weight 3):}\;Volume is the strongest signal of institutional interest or market-moving events.
  \item \textbf{Activity spike (weight 2):}\;Elevated trade frequency may indicate information asymmetry.
  \item \textbf{Price gap (weight 2):}\;Large price movements in illiquid markets warrant equal scrutiny.
\end{itemize}
\end{formulabox}

\midrule

\cellcolor{WarnAmber!15} 2 & \textcolor{WarnAmber}{\textbf{Alert}} & Single flag (activity or price gap) triggered \\
